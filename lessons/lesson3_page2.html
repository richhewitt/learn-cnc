Machine Movement in 3D Space

6-axis of movement

CNC machines move in 3D space. It is important to have a standardized way of describing that movement. Looking at the graphic above, there are three directions of linear movement: x, y, and z. There are also three rotational movements about those axis: a, b, and c.

A 5-axis CNC machine means it has 5 axis of movement, or 5 degrees of freedom. This means it can move about the x, y, and z-axis and two rotational axis.

Machine Movement

How a machine can move often dictates what types of cutting applications it is appropriate for. For instance, a 3-axis machine might not be suitable for machining complex curves and organic surfaces. However, a 5-axis machine could be overkill for processing simple geometries on panels, when a 3-axis machine can do the same thing at a fraction of the cost.
